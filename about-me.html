about Me

My name is Asadullah Hanif. I am a proud father of two wonderful children, Oswah and Mansoor. With a deep passion for cities, communities, and development, I have devoted my career to urban planning, humanitarian assistance, and academic research. I hold a Ph.D. in Urban Planning, and my journey has taken me through academia, international development organizations, and the United Nations system.

Professional Background

I have been serving as a university lecturer for several years, teaching and mentoring students in urban planning and related fields. My academic work is focused on promoting sustainable urban development, spatial planning, and community-driven solutions.

Previously, I worked with UN-Habitat as an Urban Planning Specialist for over four years. During this time, I played a key role in formulating the Strategic Action Plans for Herat and InjilMunicipalities, which were designed to guide local development and enhance urban governance. Currently, I am working with the United Nations as a Program Analyst for Humanitarian Interventions. In this role, I monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of humanitarian programs, ensuring they respond effectively to the needs of affected communities and contribute to long-term resilience.
